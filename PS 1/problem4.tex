\section*{Problem 4}

Prove that the sum of the first $n$ odd integers equals $n^2$

\noindent
Let $P(n):=1+3+5+\cdots+(2n-1)$

\noindent
Base case:\\
$P(1)=1\equiv1^2$

\noindent
Inductive case:\\
Assume $P(n)=n^2$
\begin{align*}
P(n+1)  &= 1+3+5+\cdots+2n-1+2(n+1)-1  \\
        &= 1+3+5+\cdots+2n-1+2n+1      \\
        &= n^2+2n+1                    \\
        &= (n+1)^2
\end{align*}
$\therefore P(n)=n^2$\\
$\square$
